The science of calm

Why a wrap calms when a bag panics

It's not magic and it's not force. It's the same gentle-pressure principle vets and parents have used for decades.

Deep-pressure calm

Even, all-over pressure triggers the same "swaddle" response that settles a baby — the nervous system reads it as safe and the fight drains out. A bag presses nowhere and grips everywhere, so the cat keeps struggling.

Less to panic about

Soft, quiet fabric (no scary plastic crinkle) lowers the noise and the visual chaos that set a cat off in the first place. Calmer input → calmer cat.

One limb, then done

You expose exactly the paw or area you need — not the whole cat — so the job is quick and the cat never feels fully cornered. Quick and contained beats long and total every time.

Questions, answered

"This would never work on MY cat." +
That's what most owners say — including the ones reviewing the spiciest, most fractious cats they've owned. The swaddle pressure works with a cat's instinct to feel safe when held snugly, not against it. And you're covered by our 100-day guarantee, so trying it on your cat costs you nothing.
Isn't wrapping cruel? Won't it panic more? +
Gentle wrapping is the opposite of scruffing — vets use it precisely because it calms. The fabric is soft and quiet (no scary plastic), it lowers visual stimulation, and most cats settle within a minute. We include a positive-association quick-start so the very first time builds trust instead of fear.
What size do I need? +
We make Kitten, S, M, L and XL — most adult cats are M, large or fluffy cats L/XL. Our size finder matches by weight and build. (The #1 complaint with other wraps is one-size-fits-all; we fixed that.)
Can I use it with meds my vet prescribed? +
Yes — owners use it for pills, ear drops, subQ fluids and insulin, and it works alongside calming aids like gabapentin your vet may already recommend. It simply makes home care easier. It's a grooming & calming aid, not a medical device.
